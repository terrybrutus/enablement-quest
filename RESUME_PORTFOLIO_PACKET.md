# Enablement Quest Resume And Portfolio Packet

## Positioning Statement

Enablement Quest is an RPG-style performance consulting simulation created by Terry Brutus to show how learning and enablement professionals diagnose workplace problems, choose the right intervention, and connect decisions to measurable business impact.

Short version:

> I built an interactive enablement simulation that turns performance consulting judgment into a playable case study.

## What This Proves

This project is strongest evidence for roles that value:

- Performance consulting
- Learning experience design
- Sales enablement strategy
- Instructional design with business impact
- AI-supported workflow design
- Accessibility-conscious product thinking
- Systems thinking across people, process, tools, and measurement

The core portfolio claim is not that the game is visually finished. The stronger claim is that the experience demonstrates a business-first learning architecture: diagnose first, design second, measure impact third.

- Designed enablement decision points that connect discovery guides, manager coaching rubrics, call-review practice, and pipeline inspection dashboards to demo-to-next-step conversion.
- Demonstrated the ability to connect enablement strategy to sales behavior, manager reinforcement, adoption signals, and measurable pipeline outcomes.

### Performance Consultant / Enablement Strategist

- Created an interactive performance consulting simulation that asks learners to diagnose whether a workplace problem is caused by knowledge gaps, process friction, unclear expectations, coaching gaps, or measurement failures.
- Modeled a diagnose-before-design workflow using stakeholder input, evidence collection, root-cause analysis, intervention selection, and impact mapping.
- Framed training as one possible solution inside a broader performance system rather than the default response to every workplace problem.

### AI / Workflow Portfolio Framing

- Used AI-assisted development workflows to prototype and iterate a browser-based learning simulation while keeping the learning judgment, evidence model, and portfolio positioning human-directed.
- Framed responsible AI as support for research, drafting, summarization, and iteration, while preserving human accountability for diagnosis and final enablement decisions.

## Evidence Boundaries

Use truthful phrasing when presenting this project:

- Say: "Built a browser-based RPG-style learning simulation."
- Say: "Designed the learning architecture, case flow, interaction model, and portfolio positioning."
- Say: "Used AI-assisted development workflows under human direction."
- Do not say: "Trained an AI model."
- Do not imply the current metrics are live production results. They are scenario-based impact targets unless real pilot data is collected later.
- Do not overclaim visual polish until the playable experience has passed a full visual QA pass.
